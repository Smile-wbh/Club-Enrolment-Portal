Cycling Club

Cycling Club organises city rides, weekend endurance sessions, and beginner-friendly route practice. We share route details ahead of time and focus on group safety, pacing, and confidence on the bike.

Club Overview

The club supports riders at different levels, from short confidence rides to longer endurance outings. Training emphasises safe positioning, smooth pacing, equipment checks, and clear group communication.

Venue Information

  • Meet-up point: Main campus gate, where riders gather before route checks and departure.
  • Route type: Most sessions use quieter roads or greenways and avoid heavy-traffic sections.
  • Pace groups: Rides are usually split into easy, steady, and longer-distance groups.
  • Safety rule: Helmets and working brakes are strongly recommended, and group instructions should be followed throughout.
  • Weather plan: Bad weather may move the session to indoor conditioning, maintenance tips, or an online workshop.

What We Do

  • Weekend rides: structured routes for endurance and pacing
  • Beginner support: short rides to build confidence and handling
  • Skill work: climbing rhythm, gear choice, and group spacing
  • Ride planning: route notes, regroup points, and safety reminders

Who It Is For

  • Beginners who want a safer start with short group rides
  • Riders improving endurance and sustainable pace control
  • Members who enjoy outdoor exploration and shared training goals
  • Anyone wanting regular weekend riding with clear organisation

Ride Flow (Sample)

  • Bike check: brakes, tyres, lights, and supplies (10 minutes)
  • Warm-up ride: easy rolling pace to settle in (15-20 minutes)
  • Main block: pace-group ride or endurance section (60-90 minutes)
  • Regroup and return: recovery pace plus route debrief (20-30 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: helmet, gloves, front and rear lights, water, and small snacks
  • Bike prep: road and mountain bikes are both fine if brakes and gears work reliably
  • Group etiquette: avoid sudden braking, keep spacing steady, and call hazards clearly
  • Safety: bright clothing is helpful, especially for evening rides
