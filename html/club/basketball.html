Basketball Club

Basketball Club runs weekly training and campus league preparation covering ball handling, shooting, footwork, half-court tactics, and controlled scrimmages. Members can develop from beginner fundamentals to stronger game understanding.

Club Overview

Sessions are designed to improve both individual skill and team coordination. Training normally moves through warm-up, ball work, finishing, shooting, and game scenarios so players can build habits they can use in real matches.

Venue Information

  • Main venue: Indoor Court D with full-court and half-court practice zones.
  • Check-in: Meet at the sports hall entrance around 10 minutes before the session to sign in.
  • Equipment: Basketballs are provided. Knee or ankle support is optional but recommended if needed.
  • Court rule: Indoor trainers are required and sharp accessories should be removed before play.
  • Venue updates: Any short-notice court change will be shared through club notices.

What We Do

  • Foundations: dribbling, passing, finishing, and basic shooting form
  • Skill blocks: footwork, closeouts, screens, and transition reads
  • Tactics: spacing, help defence, and simple half-court sets
  • Game play: 3v3 or 5v5 scrimmages with quick review points

Who It Is For

  • Beginners looking for a structured start in basketball
  • Regular players improving decision-making and game tempo
  • Members interested in campus leagues or friendly games
  • People who enjoy high-energy team sport sessions indoors

Training Plan (Sample)

  • Warm-up: mobility, light running, and activation (10 minutes)
  • Core drills: passing rhythm and finishing patterns (20-30 minutes)
  • Focused work: shooting footwork and contact finishing (20-30 minutes)
  • Scrimmage: grouped play plus quick debrief (30-40 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: indoor basketball shoes, towel, and water bottle
  • Safety: mention any existing ankle or knee issue before training starts
  • Respect: keep scrimmages competitive but controlled, and avoid reckless contact
  • Timing: coming early helps with sign-in, team splits, and warm-up
