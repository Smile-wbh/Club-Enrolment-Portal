Gymnastics Club

Gymnastics Club focuses on flexibility, core strength, balance, and movement control. Members can start from basic body awareness and progress into cleaner combinations, coached drills, and showcase activities.

Flexibility Work Core Strength Balance Control Showcase Practice
📍 Venue: Gymnastics Room H
Focus This Week Shoulder mobility and core stability for the beginner group.

Club Overview

Training follows a progressive and safety-first structure. Sessions build flexibility, alignment, and control step by step, with demonstrations, corrections, and small-group practice suited to each member’s level.

Venue Information

  • Main venue: Gym Room H with mats and a shared warm-up area.
  • Check-in: Please gather at the room entrance around 10 minutes early for sign-in and group setup.
  • Equipment: Mats and basic apparatus are provided; bands or wrist support are optional.
  • Room rule: Keep the floor clean, avoid sharp accessories, and follow mat-use guidance.
  • Venue updates: Any room change will be shared ahead of time through club notices.

What We Do

  • Flexibility: shoulder, back, hip, and hamstring mobility
  • Strength: core stability, support positions, and body-line control
  • Control: balance, alignment, and movement pathway drills
  • Presentation: short sequences and optional showcase preparation

Who It Is For

  • Beginners building mobility and body awareness from the ground up
  • Members improving posture, control, and stability
  • People with dance, yoga, or conditioning experience who want more structure
  • Anyone interested in showcase-based movement practice

Training Plan (Sample)

  • Warm-up: joint prep and light cardio (8-10 minutes)
  • Mobility block: shoulder and hip flexibility work (20-25 minutes)
  • Strength block: core and support drills (20-25 minutes)
  • Sequence work: basic combinations and cooldown (20-30 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: training clothes, towel, water, and optional wrist support
  • Safety: progress gradually and stop early if a stretch feels unsafe
  • Respect: leave the training area tidy and return equipment after use
  • Timing: early arrival supports a safer warm-up and group placement
