Table Tennis Club

Table Tennis Club runs indoor sessions for both new and experienced players, with training in stance, footwork, forehand and backhand control, serve-return routines, and small competitive formats.

Club Overview

The club aims to improve consistency, movement, and match confidence through regular practice. New members build a reliable technical base first, while returning players can sharpen tempo, spin handling, and tactical choices.

Venue Information

  • Main venue: Indoor Hall I with shared tables and warm-up space.
  • Check-in: Please gather about 10 minutes before the session to sign in and receive a table grouping.
  • Equipment: Loaner bats may be available for first-time attendees, and club balls are provided.
  • Hall rule: Wear suitable indoor footwear and keep table areas clear and safe.
  • Venue updates: Temporary changes are announced through club notices or group messages.

What We Do

  • Basics: forehand and backhand contact, blocks, and drives
  • Special work: serve spin, receive placement, and first-three-ball patterns
  • Movement: side-to-side footwork and balance control
  • Competition: group round robins and quick tactical review

Who It Is For

  • Beginners learning core technique and basic rules
  • Players improving consistency and live-play confidence
  • Members interested in campus events or friendly competition
  • Anyone who enjoys regular indoor practice with a social side

Training Plan (Sample)

  • Warm-up: shoulder-wrist prep and footwork activation (10 minutes)
  • Core drills: control and placement work (20-30 minutes)
  • Focused block: serve-return and early-rally patterns (20-30 minutes)
  • Match play: group games and quick debrief (30-35 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: indoor shoes, towel, water, and your own bat if possible
  • Safety: build intensity gradually to protect the wrist and shoulder
  • Courtesy: rotate tables fairly and help tidy balls and equipment after use
  • Timing: arriving early supports smoother grouping and warm-up
