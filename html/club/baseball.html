Baseball Club

Baseball Club combines fundamentals and live-play practice, including throwing, catching, batting, base running, and defensive positioning. Members can learn the basics first and then move into role-specific work and team scenarios.

Club Overview

We build sessions around clear skill progressions so new players can learn safely and more experienced members can sharpen game awareness. Training usually blends technical reps with small tactical situations and short live segments.

Venue Information

  • Main venue: Baseball Field L with a shared warm-up area and marked defensive zones.
  • Check-in: Meet by the field entrance about 10 minutes before the session for group allocation.
  • Equipment: Balls and some loaner gloves are available, but bringing your own glove is ideal.
  • Safety rule: Keep clear of batting and throwing lanes unless a coach or organiser directs you in.
  • Weather plan: If the field becomes unavailable, the club may switch to indoor review or skill work.

What We Do

  • Foundations: throwing mechanics, receiving, and footwork
  • Batting: swing path, contact point, and batting-cage style reps
  • Running: starts, turns, and basic base-running decisions
  • Game work: defensive shape, situational drills, and short innings

Who It Is For

  • Members trying baseball for the first time
  • Players who want steadier throwing, catching, and hitting basics
  • People who enjoy tactical team sports and role-based play
  • Anyone interested in friendly games and regular outdoor practice

Training Plan (Sample)

  • Warm-up: running prep and dynamic mobility (10-12 minutes)
  • Skill work: throw-catch and footwork circuits (20-30 minutes)
  • Batting block: contact drills and swing practice (20-30 minutes)
  • Live phase: situational defence plus short game play (30-40 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: trainers or cleats, glove if available, towel, and water
  • Safety: always respect the hitting area and wait for clear calls before entering
  • Care: return shared equipment neatly after use
  • Timing: early arrival helps with warm-up and position grouping
