Badminton Club

Badminton Club offers weekly practice sessions and friendly matches for players of different levels. Training covers footwork, shot quality, doubles coordination, and match play, with space for both beginners and returning players.

Club Overview

The club helps members build consistent fundamentals and confidence on court. Sessions move from warm-up and technique work into drills, rally patterns, and small match blocks so everyone can train at a suitable pace.

Venue Information

  • Main venue: Indoor Court B with standard badminton lines and shared practice courts.
  • Check-in: Arrive around 10 minutes early to sign in and join your assigned drill group.
  • Equipment: Shuttles are provided for club sessions. Bringing your own racket is recommended.
  • Court etiquette: Rotate fairly, clear used shuttles, and keep walkways open during drills.
  • Schedule changes: Any venue adjustment will be posted in advance through the booking page and club notices.

What We Do

  • Technique: grip, clears, drops, drives, and basic net play
  • Movement: split step, recovery, and front-back court coverage
  • Doubles: rotation, communication, and serve-return routines
  • Play: coached rallies, mini games, and friendly match blocks

Who It Is For

  • Beginners who want a clear starting point for regular practice
  • Intermediate players building consistency and court coverage
  • Members interested in doubles teamwork and match play
  • Anyone looking for a social indoor sport with steady weekly training

Training Plan (Sample)

  • Warm-up: mobility, shadow footwork, and activation (10 minutes)
  • Core drills: serve-receive and shot control blocks (20-30 minutes)
  • Focused play: doubles rotation and pressure rallies (20-30 minutes)
  • Match time: grouped games with quick feedback (30-40 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: indoor court shoes, racket, towel, and water bottle
  • Safety: stop early if you feel knee, ankle, or shoulder discomfort
  • Courtesy: respect court rotation and give others space during drills
  • Timing: arriving early helps with grouping, warm-up, and court allocation
