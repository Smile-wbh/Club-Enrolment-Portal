Handball Club

Handball Club centres on teamwork, fast transitions, shooting, and defensive positioning. Training is split by level so members can learn the rules first and then grow into quicker game situations and stronger coordination.

Club Overview

Handball mixes movement, contact, and quick decisions, so sessions focus on fundamentals first and then add more tactical and game-like challenges. The club also supports internal matches and friendly activity days.

Venue Information

  • Main venue: Indoor Hall G with marked handball areas and warm-up space.
  • Check-in: Meet at the sports hall entrance around 10 minutes before the activity starts.
  • Equipment: Handballs are provided; non-slip indoor shoes and optional support gear are recommended.
  • Hall rule: Remove sharp items and respect court boundaries during active drills.
  • Venue updates: Temporary venue changes are announced in advance when possible.

What We Do

  • Basics: passing, dribbling, coordination, and timing
  • Attack: breakthroughs, combination play, and wing finishing
  • Defence: shape, cover support, and group recovery
  • Play: small-area attack-defence games and short matches

Who It Is For

  • Beginners learning the rules and movement patterns
  • Members who enjoy fast-paced indoor team sports
  • Players improving coordination, fitness, and group awareness
  • Anyone ready for regular practice and organised scrimmages

Training Plan (Sample)

  • Warm-up: mobility plus passing activation (10-15 minutes)
  • Core block: footwork and shooting mechanics (20-30 minutes)
  • Tactical block: attacking patterns or defensive shape (20-30 minutes)
  • Game play: grouped matches and quick review (30-35 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: indoor shoes, towel, water, and optional hand or ankle support
  • Safety: avoid heavy pulling and uncontrolled body contact
  • Respect: follow the organiser and referee instructions during games
  • Timing: early arrival helps with grouping and warm-up
