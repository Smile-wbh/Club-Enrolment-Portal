Rugby Club

Rugby Club offers training in passing, running lines, contact awareness, and tactical play, with options for members who want to build toward inter-university fixtures. Safety and clear coaching are a core part of every session.

Club Overview

Rugby is built around teamwork, strategy, and controlled physical play. Sessions introduce the rules and core movement patterns first, then add more tactical structure and game realism as members become more confident.

Venue Information

  • Main venue: Outdoor Field F with a marked sideline warm-up zone.
  • Check-in: Gather by the field edge around 10 minutes before training for sign-in and bib distribution.
  • Safety briefing: Contact elements are introduced with coached explanations and must follow organiser instructions.
  • Equipment: Suitable field footwear is recommended, and mouthguards are optional but sensible for contact work.
  • Weather plan: Heavy rain may move the activity to indoor conditioning or tactical review.

What We Do

  • Basics: passing technique, support lines, and carrying mechanics
  • Special work: tackling shape, defensive spacing, and ball presentation
  • Tactics: restart shape and simple attacking organisation
  • Game play: conditioned drills and small-sided rugby situations

Who It Is For

  • Beginners who want to understand the rules and structure first
  • Athletes looking to improve conditioning and contact awareness
  • Members interested in team competition and role-based training
  • Students who value disciplined, coached group sport

Training Plan (Sample)

  • Warm-up: running prep and dynamic mobility (10-12 minutes)
  • Core drills: passing rhythm and support movement (20-30 minutes)
  • Focused block: defence and contact mechanics (20-30 minutes)
  • Game play: grouped scenarios and short matches (30-40 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: suitable field shoes, optional mouthguard, towel, and water
  • Safety: contact work should increase gradually and stop if something feels unsafe
  • Discipline: follow organiser instructions and respect teammates throughout drills
  • Timing: early arrival helps with briefings, grouping, and safe warm-up
