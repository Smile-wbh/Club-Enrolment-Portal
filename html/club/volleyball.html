Volleyball Club

Volleyball Club offers structured practice in passing, setting, serving, attack timing, and team systems. Sessions are designed for members who want to build fundamentals, improve communication, and enjoy organised indoor play.

Club Overview

The club helps members improve both technical consistency and team rhythm. Practice moves from individual touch quality into positional drills, small-team phases, and match-style points so everyone can train with purpose.

Venue Information

  • Main venue: Indoor volleyball court with warm-up space behind the baseline.
  • Check-in: Arrive around 10 minutes early to sign in and join your court group.
  • Equipment: Volleyballs are provided. Knee pads are optional but helpful for frequent floor work.
  • Hall rule: Indoor shoes are recommended and non-playing space should stay clear during rallies.
  • Venue updates: Temporary court changes will be shared through club notices.

What We Do

  • Foundations: platform passing, setting touch, and serving mechanics
  • Attack work: approach timing, contact, and controlled hitting
  • Team systems: receive formation, defensive coverage, and rotation basics
  • Play: small-sided rallies and full-team point sequences

Who It Is For

  • Beginners who want to learn clean touch and movement basics
  • Members improving serve-receive confidence and team communication
  • Players interested in regular indoor team practice
  • Anyone who enjoys cooperative, fast-paced group sport

Training Plan (Sample)

  • Warm-up: mobility and ball-feel activation (10 minutes)
  • Core drills: passing and setting quality work (20-30 minutes)
  • Focused block: attack timing or defensive shape (20-30 minutes)
  • Game play: structured rallies and match segments (30-35 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: indoor shoes, towel, water, and optional knee pads
  • Safety: communicate clearly to avoid collisions on shared plays
  • Respect: keep team talk constructive and rotate fairly during drills
  • Timing: early arrival supports better grouping and warm-up
