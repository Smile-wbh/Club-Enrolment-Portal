Swimming Club

Swimming Club runs regular pool sessions focused on stroke technique, breathing rhythm, pacing, and confidence in the water. Members can start with core freestyle and breaststroke work and progress into more structured swim sets.

Club Overview

The club supports swimmers who want clearer structure, better technique, and regular pool practice. Sessions balance technical breakdowns with manageable repeat sets so members can improve form and endurance together.

Venue Information

  • Main venue: Campus pool lanes with club check-in before lane allocation.
  • Check-in: Meet about 10 minutes before the session to change and join your lane group.
  • Equipment: Goggles and swim caps are recommended, and some technical drills may use kickboards or pull buoys.
  • Pool rule: Follow lane direction, leave space for others, and respect pool staff guidance.
  • Schedule updates: Any pool access change will be posted ahead of time if possible.

What We Do

  • Technique: breathing, body line, kick timing, and catch rhythm
  • Stroke work: freestyle, breaststroke, and optional progression drills
  • Fitness: repeat sets for pacing and basic endurance
  • Practice support: lane grouping, feedback, and confidence-building

Who It Is For

  • Beginners building comfort and rhythm in structured pool sessions
  • Members improving stroke efficiency and pacing control
  • Swimmers preparing for longer sets or timed efforts
  • Anyone who enjoys low-impact training with clear coaching cues

Pool Session Plan (Sample)

  • Pool warm-up: easy lengths and mobility prep
  • Technique block: breathing, kick, or stroke-specific drill work
  • Main set: repeat efforts with steady pacing targets
  • Cooldown: relaxed lengths and short technique recap

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: goggles, swim cap, towel, water, and optional training aids
  • Safety: let the organiser know about any medical concern or low confidence in the water
  • Pool etiquette: respect lane order and regroup at the wall when asked
  • Timing: arriving early helps with changing and lane assignment
