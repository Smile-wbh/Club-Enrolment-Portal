Tennis Club

Tennis Club combines stroke practice, footwork, serving, and structured hitting sessions for players at different levels. Members can build sound technique first and then move into more live rallies, match routines, and competition practice.

Club Overview

The club aims to make tennis sessions structured and accessible. Training usually blends movement prep, feeding drills, serve work, and cooperative or competitive rally blocks so players can keep improving in a clear routine.

Venue Information

  • Main venue: Tennis Court area with shared rally courts and serving space.
  • Check-in: Please meet around 10 minutes early to sign in and receive your court assignment.
  • Equipment: Bringing your own racket is recommended. Balls are provided for organised sessions.
  • Court etiquette: Rotate fairly, collect balls safely, and keep adjoining court space clear.
  • Weather plan: Outdoor sessions may be shortened or moved if court conditions become unsafe.

What We Do

  • Technique: forehand, backhand, serve, and contact-point awareness
  • Movement: split step, recovery, and positioning before the strike
  • Pattern play: rally targets, direction change, and basic point building
  • Play: partner hitting, tie-break formats, and match preparation

Who It Is For

  • Beginners who want a steady route into tennis basics
  • Intermediate players improving rally quality and serving confidence
  • Members interested in friendly matches or challenge ladders
  • Anyone who enjoys regular court sport practice outdoors

Training Plan (Sample)

  • Warm-up: mobility and shadow swings (10 minutes)
  • Core drills: fed-ball contact and movement work (20-30 minutes)
  • Serve block: toss, rhythm, and target practice (20-25 minutes)
  • Rally play: cooperative and competitive point sequences (25-35 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Recommended: suitable court shoes, racket, towel, water, and sun protection when needed
  • Safety: stop early if shoulder or elbow pain appears during serving
  • Courtesy: collect balls promptly and respect shared court rotation
  • Timing: early arrival helps with court grouping and warm-up
