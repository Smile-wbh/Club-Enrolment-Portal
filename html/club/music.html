Club Overview

The society aims to provide a consistent space for rehearsal and creative collaboration. Members can join ensemble groups, share practice ideas, and prepare for campus performances at a pace that works for their experience level.

Venue Information

  • In-person rehearsal: Practice room on Arts Building 2F, with members gathering about 10 minutes before the session.
  • Online sharing: Scores, rehearsal notes, and planning updates are shared through the club workspace.
  • Equipment: Some rooms include basic stands and audio support, but personal instruments are recommended.
  • Performance planning: Concert or event sign-up normally opens in advance with extra rehearsals arranged as needed.
  • Fallback plan: If a room is unavailable, rehearsal may move online or to another arts practice space.

What We Do

  • Ensemble practice: section coordination, timing, and group balance
  • Technique exchange: practice tips and instrument-specific discussion
  • Set preparation: covers, arrangements, and showcase pieces
  • Performance support: campus events and club-stage opportunities

Who It Is For

  • Members who enjoy ensemble rehearsal and shared music-making
  • Players wanting more confidence in rehearsal and stage settings
  • People looking to meet others through regular creative practice
  • Anyone interested in performance opportunities and collaborative projects

Rehearsal Flow (Sample)

  • Warm-up: timing, scales, or rhythm prep (10 minutes)
  • Section work: part-by-part practice and entry alignment (20-30 minutes)
  • Full run: ensemble rehearsal with balance adjustments (30-40 minutes)
  • Review: note issues and set next-session goals (10 minutes)

Equipment & Notes

  • Please arrive on time so ensemble work can start smoothly
  • Keep rehearsal rooms tidy and reset stands or chairs after use
  • Look after your own instruments and valuable personal items
  • Performance slots usually require advance sign-up and attendance at related rehearsals
